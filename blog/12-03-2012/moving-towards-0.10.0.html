Moving towards 0.10.0

Recently I announced the release of 0.7.0. In these release notes I hinted at the fact that there may be a large gap in between 0.7.0 and the next release (0.10.0). Today, I am here to announce that this is indeed the case.

Why the gap?

A large gap like this is generally used to indicate that a lot of work has been done. In this case, we have a lot of work planned. As such, by the time 0.10.0 is released, the framework wont likely look like it does now.

Why such a large re factor?

There are certain areas of Proem I haven't been happy with for quite some time. Some are over engineered, others have been neglected, while others are just poorly designed and need to be fixed. Rest assured, 0.10.0 is going to fix a lot of issues and make the framework better.

We may drop some history.

This re factor is indeed such a large change that big chunks of the current framework are being moved or removed and rewritten. In doing so we may drop some history of changes, but really once 0.10.0 is released, you wont need to know about anything prior.

I am not expecting this re factoring to take a considerable amount of time. I am hoping to get this release complete and packaged by late February. Though as always, I'm not promising anything.
